Introduction

The heat equation u_t = k·u_xx models heat diffusion, Brownian motion, and many physical processes. Unlike the wave equation, it is irreversible (parabolic), has infinite speed of propagation, and instantly smooths initial data. Solutions decay exponentially as t→∞.

IBVP & Separation of Variables

Heat IBVP (finite rod)
u_t = k·u_xx, 0 < x < L, t > 0 u(0,t) = 0, u(L,t) = 0 (Dirichlet BCs) u(x,0) = f(x) (initial condition)
Separation u(x,t)=X(x)T(t)

T′/kT = X″/X = −λ. BCs: X(0)=X(L)=0 ⟹ λₙ=(nπ/L)², Xₙ=sin(nπx/L). Then T_n(t)=e^(−kλₙt) = e^(−k(nπ/L)²t).

Full Solution
u(x,t) = Σ_{n=1}^∞ bₙ sin(nπx/L) e^{−k(nπ/L)²t} bₙ = (2/L)∫₀ᴸ f(x)sin(nπx/L)dx

Each mode decays exponentially. The dominant term as t→∞ is b₁sin(πx/L)e^(−k(π/L)²t).

Heat Kernel (Fundamental Solution)

Heat Kernel on ℝ
K(x,t) = (1/√(4πkt)) · e^{−x²/(4kt)}, t > 0

Properties: K(x,t)>0, ∫_{-∞}^∞ K(x,t)dx=1 (it's a Gaussian/probability distribution), K→δ(x) as t→0⁺.

Solution via Heat Kernel (Cauchy Problem on ℝ)
u(x,t) = ∫_{-∞}^∞ K(x−y,t) f(y) dy = (K*f)(x) (convolution in x)

This is valid for bounded continuous f. Note: u is instantly C^∞ for t>0 even if f is discontinuous — the smoothing effect.

Infinite Speed of Propagation

Even if f is compactly supported, u(x,t)>0 for ALL x immediately (t>0). This contrasts with the wave equation's finite speed c.

Maximum Principle

Weak Maximum Principle

Let u satisfy u_t=ku_xx in Q_T=(0,L)×(0,T]. Then max u attained on the parabolic boundary Γ_T = {t=0} ∪ {x=0} ∪ {x=L}.

max_{Q̄_T} u = max_{Γ_T} u

Similarly for the minimum. Consequence: if u=0 on parabolic boundary, then u≡0 in Q_T.

Consequences
  • Uniqueness: IBVP has at most one solution.
  • Comparison: if u≤v on Γ_T then u≤v in Q_T.
  • Stability: max|u|≤max_{Γ_T}|f|+T·max|source|.
Strong Maximum Principle

If u attains its maximum at an interior point, then u is constant. (Irreversibility — heat diffuses, cannot concentrate.)

Non-Homogeneous & Steady State

Steady-State Solution

For u_t=ku_xx with u(0,t)=T₁, u(L,t)=T₂: steady state satisfies u_ss″=0 ⟹ u_ss=T₁+(T₂−T₁)x/L (linear).

Write u=u_ss+v; then v satisfies homogeneous BCs and decays to 0.

Non-Homogeneous Source (Duhamel's Principle)
u_t = k·u_xx + F(x,t), u(x,0)=0 u(x,t) = ∫₀^t v(x,t−s;s) ds

where v(x,τ;s) solves the homogeneous heat equation with IC v(x,0;s)=F(x,s).

Summary

Separation

u=Σbₙsin(nπx/L)e^{−k(nπ/L)²t}. bₙ=(2/L)∫f·sin.

Heat Kernel

K=(4πkt)^{−½}e^{−x²/4kt}. u=K*f. Normalized Gaussian.

Smoothing

u∈C^∞ for t>0. Infinite speed of propagation (K>0 everywhere).

Max Principle

max u = max on parabolic boundary Γ_T. Gives uniqueness and stability.

Decay

All modes decay: e^{−kλₙt}→0. Dominant mode n=1.

Non-Homogeneous

Duhamel: u=∫₀^t v(x,t−s;s)ds. Steady state = linear profile.
