Introduction

The field ℂ of complex numbers extends ℝ by adjoining i = √(−1). Every polynomial over ℂ splits (Fundamental Theorem of Algebra). The complex plane ℂ ≅ ℝ² inherits a rich topology that underpins all of complex analysis.

Arithmetic of ℂ

Complex Numbers

z = x + iy where x = Re(z) (real part), y = Im(z) (imaginary part), i² = −1.

Conjugate: z̄ = x − iy. Modulus: |z| = √(x²+y²) = √(zz̄). Argument: arg(z) = θ where z = |z|(cos θ + i sin θ).

Operations

  • Addition: (a+bi)+(c+di) = (a+c)+(b+d)i
  • Multiplication: (a+bi)(c+di) = (ac−bd)+(ad+bc)i
  • Division: z/w = zw̄/|w|² (multiply by conjugate)
  • |zw| = |z||w|, arg(zw) = arg z + arg w

Modulus Identities

  • |z+w| ≤ |z|+|w| (triangle inequality)
  • ||z|−|w|| ≤ |z−w|
  • |z|² = zz̄
  • Re(z) ≤ |z|, Im(z) ≤ |z|
Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

Every non-constant polynomial p(z) ∈ ℂ[z] of degree n has exactly n roots in ℂ (counted with multiplicity). Equivalently, ℂ is algebraically closed.

Polar Form & Arguments

Polar & Exponential Form

Polar form: z = r(cos θ + i sin θ) = re^(iθ), where r = |z| ≥ 0 and θ = arg(z).

Euler's formula: e^(iθ) = cos θ + i sin θ.

Principal argument: Arg(z) ∈ (−π, π]. Note arg(z) is multi-valued: arg(z) = Arg(z) + 2πk, k ∈ ℤ.

De Moivre's Theorem
(cos θ + i sin θ)ⁿ = cos(nθ) + i sin(nθ) for all n ∈ ℤ

Equivalently: (e^(iθ))ⁿ = e^(inθ). Used to express cos(nθ), sin(nθ) in terms of cos θ, sin θ.

Topology of ℂ

Basic Topological Notions

Open disc: D(z₀,r) = {z : |z−z₀| < r}. Closed disc: D̄(z₀,r) = {z : |z−z₀| ≤ r}.

Interior point: z₀ ∈ S is interior if some D(z₀,r) ⊆ S.

Open set: Every point is interior. Closed set: Contains all its limit points (complement is open).

Boundary: ∂S = S̄ ∩ (ℂ\S)̄ = {z : every D(z,r) meets both S and ℂ\S}.

PropertyDefinition
BoundedS ⊆ D(0,M) for some M > 0
CompactClosed and bounded (Heine-Borel in ℂ ≅ ℝ²)
ConnectedCannot be written as union of two disjoint non-empty open sets
Polygonally connectedAny two points joined by a polygonal path in S
DomainOpen and connected set
RegionDomain (possibly with some boundary points)
Simply connectedDomain with no "holes" — every closed curve is contractible in S
Important Example

The punctured plane ℂ\{0} is connected but NOT simply connected (the unit circle is not contractible to a point in ℂ\{0}). The plane ℂ itself is simply connected.

Riemann Sphere (Extended Complex Plane)

ℂ̂ = ℂ ∪ {∞}

The Riemann sphere ℂ̂ (or ℙ¹ℂ, the complex projective line) is the one-point compactification of ℂ. We adjoin a single point ∞.

Stereographic projection: Maps the unit sphere S² ⊆ ℝ³ to ℂ̂. North pole N = (0,0,1) ↦ ∞. Points on the equator map to the unit circle |z|=1.

Metric on ℂ̂Formula
Chordal metricd(z,w) = 2|z−w|/√((1+|z|²)(1+|w|²)) for z,w ∈ ℂ
d(z,∞)2/√(1+|z|²)
Key Property

ℂ̂ is compact. A sequence (zₙ) in ℂ converges to ∞ in ℂ̂ iff |zₙ|→∞. Möbius transformations (studied in T03) extend to bijections ℂ̂ → ℂ̂.

Roots & Powers

n-th Roots of Unity

The n-th roots of unity are ωₖ = e^(2πik/n), k = 0, 1, ..., n−1. They form the vertices of a regular n-gon inscribed in the unit circle. The primitive root is ω₁ = e^(2πi/n).

n-th Roots of z₀

The n-th roots of z₀ = r·e^(iθ) are:

wₖ = r^(1/n) · e^(i(θ + 2πk)/n), k = 0, 1, ..., n−1

There are exactly n distinct n-th roots (forming a regular n-gon).

Complex Logarithm

log(z) = ln|z| + i·arg(z) (multi-valued). Principal branch: Log(z) = ln|z| + i·Arg(z) (defined for z ≠ 0, with Arg ∈ (−π,π]).

Branch cut of Log: the negative real axis (where Arg has a jump from −π to π).
