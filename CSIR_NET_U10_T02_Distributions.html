Poisson Approximation to Binomial

If n→∞, p→0, np→λ, then Bin(n,p)→Poisson(λ). Rule of thumb: n≥100, p≤0.01.

Memoryless Property (Geometric)
P(X>m+n | X>m) = P(X>n)

The geometric distribution is the only discrete memoryless distribution.

Memoryless Property (Exponential)
P(X>s+t | X>s) = P(X>t)

The exponential is the only continuous memoryless distribution. Models time between Poisson events.

Normal Distribution Properties

Standard Normal Z~N(0,1)
φ(z) = (1/√(2π))e^{−z²/2} (PDF) Φ(z) = ∫_{−∞}^z φ(t)dt (CDF, no closed form) Key values: Φ(1.645)≈0.95, Φ(1.96)≈0.975, Φ(2.576)≈0.995
Linear Transformations
  • X~N(μ,σ²) ⟹ aX+b~N(aμ+b, a²σ²)
  • X~N(μ₁,σ₁²), Y~N(μ₂,σ₂²) independent ⟹ X+Y~N(μ₁+μ₂, σ₁²+σ₂²)
  • Z=(X−μ)/σ~N(0,1) (standardisation)
  • Z²~χ²(1); if Z₁,…,Zₙ iid N(0,1) then ΣZᵢ²~χ²(n)
Relationships Between Distributions
  • Exponential(λ) = Gamma(1,1/λ)
  • χ²(k) = Gamma(k/2, 2)
  • If X~Gamma(α,β), Y~Gamma(γ,β) independent: X+Y~Gamma(α+γ,β)
  • Poisson(λ) limit of Bin(n,λ/n)
  • Normal limit of Bin and Poisson (by CLT)

Limit Theorems

Weak Law of Large Numbers (WLLN)

If X₁,X₂,… iid with E[X]=μ, Var(X)<∞:

X̄ₙ = (X₁+…+Xₙ)/n →^P μ (convergence in probability)

Proof via Chebyshev: P(|X̄ₙ−μ|≥ε)≤Var(X)/(nε²)→0.

Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

If X₁,X₂,… iid with E[X]=μ, Var(X)=σ²<∞:

√n(X̄ₙ−μ)/σ →^d N(0,1) (convergence in distribution)

Remarkable: works for any distribution with finite mean and variance. Used to justify normal approximations.

Modes of Convergence
  • Almost sure: Xₙ→X a.s. (SLLN: X̄ₙ→μ a.s. under E[|X|]<∞)
  • In probability: for all ε>0, P(|Xₙ−X|>ε)→0
  • In distribution: Fₙ(x)→F(x) at all continuity points of F
  • In Lp: E[|Xₙ−X|^p]→0
  • a.s. ⟹ in probability ⟹ in distribution (none reverse in general)
